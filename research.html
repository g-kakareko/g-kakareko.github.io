Research

My primary research focuses on risk assessment and loss estimation. I try to combine all the experience I got from different departments starting from the structural mechanics and computer aided engineering, civil engineering department, and computer science. The best description of my research is the work that was published but in addition to that I am adding a short summary below:

Machine learning in loss estimation

Convolutional Neuron Network to estimate the consequence of the tree failure due to high winds, focusing on the road closure and the debris generation. Satellite images were used as the input for the Convolutional Neuron Networks (CNN) to recognize the locations and types of the trees that can fail.

Single home loss estimation

A Bayesian capacity model was used to utilize limited experimental data and the analytical estimation. The model in many cases allowed a better description of the capacity, which improved the house loss estimation.

Regional loss estimation

Florida has distinctively different regions, in terms of population density and hurricane risk. The main problem is to understand characteristics of hurricane loss in different regions of Florida, which may help to find the best mitigation strategies for a certain region.

Hurricane risk assessment

Time-dependent reliability modeling and optimal maintenance planning for residential buildings subject to hurricane winds.
